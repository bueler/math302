\problem{9}  The rate at which an object cools depends on its exposed surface area $S$, among other things.  We can include this effect in Newton's law of cooling:
	$$\frac{dT}{dt} = k S (T_m-T).$$
Here $k$, $S$, and $T_m$ are all constant, and $k$ and $S$ are positive.

\begin{subproblems}
\item Find the general solution $T(t)$.
\vfill

\item Consider water bottles with water at initial temperature $10^\circ$ C which are put outside when it is winter in Fairbanks, say at $-20^\circ$ C.  Use this information to give an updated formula $T(t)$.  (\textsl{Hint.  Your formula will still have constants $k$ and $S$ unknown.})
\vspace{2.0in}
\end{subproblems}

\problem{8}  Consider this differential equation, and these functions:
	$$y'' - y' - 12 y = 0; \qquad e^{-3x}, \quad e^{4x}$$

\end{subproblems}
